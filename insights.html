Wellbeing

Mental Wellness Indicators

Sleep Hygiene

7–9 hours of quality sleep is the single most impactful lifestyle change for mental wellness. Disrupted sleep patterns double the risk of depressive episodes.

  • Maintain a consistent sleep schedule
  • Avoid screens 60 min before bed
  • Keep bedroom cool and dark

Social Connection

Strong social bonds reduce depression risk by up to 50%. Even one deep connection can serve as a powerful mental health anchor during stressful periods.

  • Spend time with peers weekly
  • Join a study circle or club
  • Reach out to family regularly

Physical Activity

30 minutes of moderate aerobic activity daily increases serotonin and endorphin levels, producing measurable reductions in anxiety and depression symptoms.

  • Walk or cycle for 30 min daily
  • Try yoga or stretching routines
  • Take active breaks during study

Nutrition & Diet

A diet rich in omega-3s, B vitamins, and antioxidants supports brain health. Skipping meals or relying on ultra-processed foods correlates with higher depression scores.

  • Eat regular balanced meals
  • Include fruits and vegetables daily
  • Limit caffeine and alcohol

Academic Productivity Analysis

How academic habits interact with mental health outcomes in student populations.

Optimal Study Hours

4–6 hrs/day

Studies show 4–6 focused study hours daily outperforms 10+ hours of distracted study while significantly reducing burnout risk.

CGPA & Mental Health

7.0–8.5 sweet spot

Both very low and very high CGPA correlate with elevated stress. Students in the 7–8.5 range report the best balance of achievement and wellbeing.

Break Intervals

25 + 5 min cycle

The Pomodoro technique (25 min focus + 5 min break) improves retention and reduces the cognitive fatigue that fuels academic pressure scores.
