Our Mission

DepriSense was created to bridge the gap between clinical mental health screening and accessible technology. By applying state-of-the-art machine learning to student wellness data, we enable early identification of depression risk — when intervention is most effective. This tool is open, transparent, and entirely educational.

Understanding SMOTE

Real-world depression datasets are heavily imbalanced — far more students fall into the "Low Risk" category than "High Risk". Without correction, models learn to predict "Low" for almost everything, achieving high accuracy while completely failing at the critical high-risk cases.

SMOTE (Synthetic Minority Oversampling Technique) resolves this by creating new synthetic training examples for minority classes. For each minority-class sample, SMOTE selects k nearest neighbours and generates new points along the line segments connecting them in feature space — not simply duplicating existing samples.

Moderate: 18%
High: 10%
After SMOTE
